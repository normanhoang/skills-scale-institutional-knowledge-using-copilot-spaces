# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to OctoAcme's project management documentation. This folder contains comprehensive process guides designed to help teams run projects systematically, transparently, and with a focus on continuous improvement.

## Summary of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme runs projects through a clear, iterative, and data-informed five-phase lifecycle. Projects begin with **Initiation**, where a one-pager and stakeholder alignment validate the business need and define success metrics. The **Planning** phase breaks work into shippable increments with a prioritized backlog, defined acceptance criteria, and a release plan. During **Execution**, teams maintain a steady rhythm with daily standups, twice-weekly delivery syncs, and sprint-based iterations, supported by comprehensive quality assurance practices including unit tests, integration tests, and security scanning. The **Release** phase emphasizes deployment readiness, rollback planning, and post-deployment verification to minimize production risk. Finally, **Retrospectives** capture learnings and convert them into tracked action items, closing the loop on continuous improvement.

Throughout this lifecycle, clear roles and accountability are paramount. **Project Managers (PMs)** coordinate delivery, manage schedules, risks, and communications. **Product Managers (PdMs)** define outcomes, prioritize the backlog, and measure success. **Developers** implement features with a focus on quality and testability. **QA/Testing** validates that work meets acceptance criteria. This structure, combined with a weekly PM-PdM sync, monthly stakeholder updates, and a culture of psychological safety, ensures alignment across all stakeholders and enables teams to deliver customer value reliably.

Risk management and communication are woven throughout the process. OctoAcme maintains a live Risk Register to identify, assess, and mitigate threats early, with regular monitoring during weekly syncs. Stakeholders receive consistent updates via weekly status templates and escalation paths that move from team-level triage to sponsor-level involvement. This proactive approach, combined with a blameless retrospective culture and a commitment to measuring outcomes, allows teams to adapt quickly and learn from each project.

## How to Use These Docs

- **For new team members:** Start with the [Project Management Overview](./octoacme-project-management-overview.md) and [Roles & Personas](./octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) to understand OctoAcme's approach and your role.
- **For project leaders:** Keep the Project Charter (One-pager) updated in your project repository. Use the [Initiation Guide](./octoacme-project-initiation.md) to kick off new work and the [Planning](./octoacme-project-planning.md) guide to organize your backlog.
- **For developers and QA:** Reference the [Execution & Tracking](./octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) guide for PR workflows, testing expectations, and quality standards.
- **For context in Copilot Spaces:** Copy relevant process docs into your project's `.copilot/` folder to provide AI assistants with institutional knowledge and ensure consistent guidance.
- **Staying current:** Update this README and process documents whenever your team's processes change. Version control is your friend.

## Key Principles

OctoAcme's project management approach is built on these core principles:

- **Customer-first:** Prioritize customer value and usability in every decision.
- **Iterative delivery:** Deliver small, testable increments rather than big-bang releases.
- **Clear ownership:** Every project has named leads and clear role definitions.
- **Data-informed decisions:** Measure impact and iterate based on evidence and metrics.
- **Psychological safety:** Encourage feedback, learning, and blameless retrospectives.
